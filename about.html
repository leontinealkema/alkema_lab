Overview

We are passionate about collaborating with substantive experts to improve estimation methods. To that end, we collaborate with various United Nations groups, the WHO, and other organizations in the areas of global health and family planning.

Projects

Area Group/initiantive Main methodological contributions
Family planning UNPD, FP2020 The family planning estimation tool (FPET) is being used ot provide estimates and projections of contraceptive use and unmet need for contraceptives by the UNPD since 2013 and for annual reporting for the FP2020 intiative.
Child mortality UN IGME, led by UNICEF The Bayesian B-spline bias-adjustment method (B3) has been used since 2013. The UN IGME report also uses Bayesian hierachical time series models for estimating the neonatal mortality rate and the sex differences in under five mortality.
Maternal mortality UN MMEIG, led by WHO The Bayesian maternal mortality model (BMat) was used in 2015 to provide estimates of maternal mortality ratio.
Fertility UNPD The projection method for the total fertility rate has been used in the World Population Prospects, the flagship UN publication on population estimates and projections, since 2011.
