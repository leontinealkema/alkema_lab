We are passionate about developing statistical models and tools to improve estimates of demographic and health indicators worldwide, especially related to reproductive, maternal, neonatal, and child health. To that end, we collaborate with various international groups in the areas of global health and family planning.


Area Group/initiative Selected methodological contributions to global monitoring of demographic and health indicators
Family planning UNPD, FP2020 (Track20) The family planning estimation tool (FPET) is used to provide estimates and projections of contraceptive use and unmet need for contraceptives by the UNPD since 2013 and for annual reporting for the FP2020 intiative.
Abortion Guttmacher and WHO A Bayesian demographic accounting model is used to estimate incidence of abortion and unintended pregnancies.
Child mortality UN IGME, led by UNICEF The Bayesian B-spline bias-adjustment method (B3) is used to estimate the under-five mortality rate for all countries, as well as mortality at older ages; A Bayesian sparse regression model with temporal smoothing is used to estimate stillbirth rates .
Maternal mortality UN MMEIG, led by WHO The Bayesian maternal mortality model (BMat) is used to estimate the maternal mortality ratio; the Bayesian bivariate random walk model (BMis) is used for estimating sensitivity and specificity in reporting of maternal mortality in vital registration data.
